Medlert

Problem:

Medlert came to Fresh Tilled Soil with a current product that aims to be your personal 911 assistant on your smart phone. Through my time with Fresh Tilled Soil, we have done a UX deep dive to really understand the users and how they experience emergencies. Through the deep dive process, we identified the competitive landscape, design challenges, heuristic analysis, current onboarding process, personas,customers, users and pricing strategy. We looked at everything.

Solution:

What we found was that their current iPhone app and product was loaded with features that should not be there. Since Medlert is going to compete with 911 and other lifeline services, Medlert needs to be just as fast and reliable. The app needs to do one thing and do it better than 911 and thats launch an emergency faster and provide data on the user. The presentation on our research can be found here Medlert Design Strategy.

Wahdle

Problem:

Having to coordinate with family and friends over email about your trip and sharing information is tedious and cumbersome.

Solution:

Wahdle takes an “Evite.com” meets “Expedia.com” approach to solving your group travel needs.I handled the lead for all of the development and design. I was able to delegate and receive help as needed from both front and back end development. The UX and research was done by myself and I took my findings and outsourced to some of the most talented designers I have worked with to date. I managed the designers and the end result is the current design you see. A fantastic project that I will never forgot.
